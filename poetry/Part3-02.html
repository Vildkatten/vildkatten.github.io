Obsessed by Two

I’m obsessed by the number two.

If you wonder why I’m always wearing layers,
it’s because I have to get dressed twice.

My weight goes up and down,
as I either take two bites
or have two portions.

It’s the same with drink:
two sips or two pints
two glasses or two bottles.

I’ve got two houses:
one for parties
and one for quiet nights in.
And if the toilet is blocked
(because of a number two),
I can use the loo next door.

My two jobs keep me busy:
I write novels for pleasure
and perform poetry for pain.
Neither pays my bills.

Two friends are always by my side:
one agrees with everything I say,
the other tells me what I need to hear.

I’ve got two partners:
A man for love
and a woman for sex.
They argued for two days,
when they found out I was two-timing them.
Now I’m having a double wedding
on the second of the second.

Two parents, two siblings, two pets,
although one is a cat and one is a dog.
It messes with my brain a bit,

but not as much as the fact
that there’s only one of me
and I’ve only got one life.
